Given a basis \(\{e_1,\ldots,e_n\}\) of a Lie algebra \(\mathfrak{g}\text{,}\) the structure constants \(c_{i,j}^k\) defined by

\begin{equation*} c_{i,j}^k:=e_k^*([e_i,e_j]) \end{equation*}

determine the product. For instance, the multiplication table of de Graaf's 6-dimensional nilpotent Lie algebra \(L_{6,16}\) is given by

\begin{equation*} [e_1,e_2]=e_3,\;[e_1,e_3]=e_4,\;[e_1,e_4]=e_5,\;[e_2,e_5]=e_6,\;[e_3,e_4]=-e_6. \end{equation*}

This means that the nonzero structure constants of \(L_{6,16}\) are

\begin{equation*} c_{1,2}^3=c_{1,3}^4=c_{1,4}^5=c_{2,5}^6=1,\quad c_{3,4}^6=-1 \end{equation*}

and

\begin{equation*} c_{2,1}^3=c_{3,1}^4=c_{4,1}^5=c_{5,2}^6=-1,\quad c_{4,3}^6=1. \end{equation*}
